Nikola Tesla

1896-1947

Man ahead of it's time

Potrait of Nikola Tesla, 1896

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

The time line of Nikola Tesla

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Crotia.
  • 1870 - Studied engineering and physics, gaining practical experience in the early working in telephony and at Continental Edison.
  • 1884 - Emigrated to the United States
  • 1887 - Tesla and Alfred S.Brown together formed the Tesla Electric Company.
  • 1891 - On 30 July, aged 35, Tesla became a naturalized citizen of the United States. In the same year, he patented his Tesla coil.
  • 1890 - Tesla experimented with transmitting power by inductive and capacitive coupling using high AC voltages generated with his Tesla coil. He attempted to develop a wireless lighting system based on near-field inductive and capacitive coupling.
  • 1898 - Tesla demonstrated a boat that used a coherer-based radio control—which he dubbed "telautomaton"—to the public during an electrical exhibition at Madison Square Garden
  • 1901 - Tesla built Wardenclyffe tower, a powerful transmitter which is ideally for viable system of wireless transmission.
  • 1905 - The Wardenclyffe project came to a halt due to the financial problems and other events.
  • 1915 - Tesla mortagaged the Wardenclyffe property to cover his debts. Later, the tower was demolished by the new owner.
  • 1922 - He moved to the St. Regis Hotel and followed a pattern from then on of moving to a different hotel every few years and leaving unpaid bills behind.
  • 1934 - Tesla moved to the Hotel New Yorker. At this time Westinghouse Electric & Manufacturing Company began paying him $125 per month in addition to paying his rent.
  • 1943 - On 7 January, at the age of 86, Tesla died in Room 3327 of the Hotel New Yorker.
